Common Questions About the Colombian Retirement Visa

What are the specific requirements for the Colombian Pensioner Visa?

  • Pension Certification: Certification proving monthly pension payments to the applicant, with an amount not less than three (3) legal minimum monthly wages in Colombia ($5,240,646 COP, or approximately 1,435 USD, based on the January 2026 exchange rate) This certification must be issued by the competent authority or by the diplomatic or consular mission accredited in Colombia from the country where the pension is received. It must be issued no more than 3 months before the visa application and must be apostilled or legalized as necessary. If in a language other than Spanish, it must be translated into Spanish.
  • Criminal Background Check: A certificate of judicial, criminal, or police background issued by the authority of the country where the applicant has resided for the last three (3) years, with apostille or legalization and official translation.
  • Medical Certificate: A medical certificate issued by a Colombian authority or by the health authority of the country of origin, confirming the applicant's physical and psychological fitness.
  • Travel All-risk Assistance Insurance: This is not a standard medical insurance; it must be an "all risks" policy covering accidents, illness, maternity, disability, hospitalization, death, and fundamentally, repatriation of remains. The policy must guarantee coverage in Colombia for the entire duration of your visa.

How long does the visa process typically take?

Once all documents are correctly gathered and submitted, the Colombian government usually takes 2 to 5 weeks to process the visa. Please note that document gathering (apostilles, translations, etc.) can add several weeks to this overall timeline.

Is health insurance mandatory for the Pensioner Visa?

Yes. You must have a Travel All-risk Assistance Insurance. It must include accidents, illness, maternity, disability, hospitalization, death, and fundamentally, repatriation of remains. The policy must guarantee coverage in Colombia for the entire duration of your visa.

Does the visa allow me to access the public health system (EPS)?

No, the Pensioner Visa does not grant access to the public health system. You can access Colombia's excellent medical network by paying full private rates for services. Alternatively, you can contract a private local health insurance plan. Colombian Passport can assist you through our network of expert partners specialized in global assistance for foreigners.
