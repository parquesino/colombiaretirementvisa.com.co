Deep Dive: Detailed Visa Requirements & FAQ

What are the specific requirements for the Colombian Pensioner Visa?

Pension Certification

Certification proving monthly pension payments to the applicant, with an amount not less than three (3) legal minimum monthly wages in Colombia ($5,240,646 COP, or approximately 1,435 USD, based on the January 2026 exchange rate). Must be apostilled and officially translated to Spanish.

Criminal Background Check

A certificate of judicial, criminal, or police background issued by the authority of the country where the applicant has resided for the last three (3) years. It must be apostilled and officially translated.
Attention US Citizens: You must provide the federal FBI Identity History Summary. State or local police certificates are strictly NOT accepted by the Colombian government.

Medical Certificate

A medical certificate issued by a Colombian authority or by the health authority of the country of origin, confirming physical and psychological fitness.

Travel All-risk Assistance Insurance

An "all risks" policy covering accidents, illness, maternity, disability, hospitalization, death, and repatriation of remains. Must guarantee coverage in Colombia for the entire visa duration.

Can I apply if I am already in Colombia on a tourist permit?

Yes, you can apply for the Pensioner Visa while physically in Colombia on a valid tourist permit. However, it is crucial that your foreign documents are properly apostilled. Specifically, you must apostille your criminal background check (the FBI Identity History Summary for US citizens) and your official pension letter. Without these apostilled documents, your application will be rejected.

Can I request the FBI background check while I am in Colombia?

Yes, you can. However, to do so, you must have your fingerprints taken physically here in Colombia (on standard FBI fingerprint cards) and mail them to the United States in order to generate the certificate and subsequently request the apostille.

Does the Pensioner Visa allow me to bring my family (beneficiaries)?

Yes. The Pensioner Visa allows you to apply for Beneficiary Visas for your dependents. This includes your spouse or permanent partner, children under 25 years old who are economically dependent on you, or children of any age if they have a certified disability. They will receive a visa linked to the validity of your primary Pensioner Visa.

Does the Pensioner Visa count towards Permanent Residency (Resident Visa)?

Yes. The Pensioner Visa is a Type M (Migrant) visa. It allows you to accumulate time towards permanent residency. After holding this visa continuously for 5 years, you are eligible to apply for the Type R (Resident) Visa, granting you permanent status in Colombia.

What are the exact government fees for this visa?

The Colombian government charges in two stages. First, a Study Fee of $54 USD must be paid when submitting the application. Once the visa is approved, you must pay an Issuance Fee of $270 USD. Additionally, once in Colombia, the Foreigner ID Card (Cédula de Extranjería) costs approximately $60 USD. The total government cost is roughly $384 USD.

  • If you hire our legal firm: We recommend starting 7 weeks before your trip or before your current tourist permit expires. Is the Travel All-risk Assistance Insurance mandatory?

Yes. You must have a Travel All-risk Assistance Insurance. It must include accidents, illness, maternity, disability, hospitalization, death, and fundamentally, repatriation of remains. The policy must guarantee coverage in Colombia for the entire duration of your visa.

Does the visa allow me to access the public health system (EPS)?

No, the Pensioner Visa does not grant access to the public health system. You can access Colombia's excellent medical network by paying full private rates for services. Alternatively, you can contract a private local health insurance plan. Colombian Passport can assist you through our network of expert partners specialized in global assistance for foreigners.
